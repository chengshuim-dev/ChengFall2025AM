Suillus granulatus, commonly known as the drooling bolete or granulated bolete, is a porcini mushroom of the genus Suillus in the family Suillaceae. It is similar to its close relative, \Suillus luteus, but can be distinguished by its ringless stem. This species is usually found in symbiosis with pine trees and is edible. (source:Wikipedia)

"The orange-brown, to brown-yellow cap is viscid (sticky) when wet, and shiny when dry, and is usually 4 to 12 cm in diameter. "
--Source:Wikipedia
