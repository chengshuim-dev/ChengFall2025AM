Tropical Fruits Eating Guide

Fruit Edible Skin Taste Texture
Mango No Sweet Soft
Durian No Sweet & Rich Soft & Creamy
Dragon Fruit No Slightly Sweet Soft
Banana No Sweet Soft
Coconut No (except young coconut skin) Sweet & Nutty Hard / Juicy
Pineapple No Sweet & Sour Juicy / Fibrous
Pomelo No Sweet Juicy

Method of consumption

  • Many tropical fruits such as bananas, mangoes and papayas can be eaten directly after peeling.
  • Juice various tropical fruits to make delicious fruit drinks, such as mango juice, pineapple juice or mixed fruit juice.
  • Cut the tropical fruits into pieces and mix them with vegetables to make a colorful fruit salad.
  • Many tropical fruits can be used for baking, such as making banana bread with bananas, or creating pineapple cakes with pineapples.
  • Cut the tropical fruits into pieces, freeze them, and then they can be used to make ice cream or cold drinks.
